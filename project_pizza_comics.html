Pizza Comics

My friend Ivan and his wife Francesca are the owners of a pizzeria restaurant in my town. Next year they want to expand their business and provide more tables for their customers, as well showing the quality of their food. They want to have a website where customers can book a table and view the high quality of their ingredients. I helped them achieving it by creating a mobile high-fidelity prototype for that purpose. This me the process I followed was a bit shorter than for my other project, but it was also much faster.

My Process

Research

In the market research phase, I did a competitor analysis and benchmarking to find out how other companies are solving the same problem.

Wireframes

For this project I skipped directly to the sketching phase. I've drawn the low-fidelity wireframes of the booking process with paper and pen. I made two different versions, to be later compared with a A/B testing method.

Prototype & Usability Testing

I made the wireframes into interactive prototypes and tested them with real users, to find out what works or not.

I ran a usability test with 5 people, in five separate 1:1 interviews, in person as well by remote.

Conclusion

There were five key learnings from this iteration of the design process:

  1. On Solution B the users confused the "Book a table" button as part of the picture.
  2. Various users felt the surname as unnecessary, they never got asked about it when booking by phone.
  3. Users usually preferred the version of the calendary shown in Solution B, it's easier to see the selected day.
  4. Users felt better when they could change their reservation after completing the booking process.
  5. Users felt the homepage of Solution A as less cramped and more clean.

I will use these learnings for later versions of the prototype.
